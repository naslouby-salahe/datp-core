14. **Why this ticket exists.** Reuse and invalidation decisions throughout the later planner/reuse-gate machinery depend on knowing exactly what kind of scientific or operational meaning changed between two resolved configurations, not merely that some nested field differs — Architecture §11.5 states this explicitly: `ResolvedSpecDiffer.compare` is pure and compares two fully mapped specifications field-by-field through typed `ScientificSpecificationChange`, `ExecutionPolicyChange`, and `EnvironmentChange` variants, never a recursive dictionary diff. Each change carries its affected stage identities and exactly one `ReuseImpact` — `TRAINING_INVALIDATED`, `SCORING_INVALIDATED`, `THRESHOLD_INVALIDATED`, `EVALUATION_STATISTICS_INVALIDATED`, or `NO_OUTPUT_IMPACT` (Architecture §6.3's domain-owned lifecycle vocabulary) — so a threshold-only change is classified distinctly from a machine-path change that alters nothing scientific. `ResolvedConfigurationArtifact` records the fully mapped configuration itself, with an identity and schema version, so a later diff always compares two genuinely persisted, addressable resolved configurations rather than two in-memory objects of uncertain provenance. The diff never mutates a spec and never itself unlocks reuse (Architecture §11.5's closing sentence) — it is read by `composition` to render a pre-run summary and by the later reuse gate (P1-T036) and lineage audit (P5-T005) as a typed input, not an authority that grants reuse on its own.
